I am a third-year undergraduate student in Computer Science and Technology at Lanzhou University, fortunate to be advised by Associate Prof. Zhongfeng Kang. My research lies at the intersection of trustworthy deep learning and scientific discovery, with a particular interest in embedding physical, topological, and dynamical priors into neural architectures so that they deliver interpretable and reliable solutions on safety-critical tasks.

To date, I have 4 first/co-first-author SCI Q1 papers accepted or published in Knowledge-Based Systems, Expert Systems with Applications, and Neurocomputing, together with 3 invention patents currently under substantive examination at CNIPA. I have also served as a core member of two National-Level College Student Innovation and Entrepreneurship Programs.

Research interests: Agentic AI, Explainability Analysis, AI for Science (AI4Science), Efficient Multi-Modal Reasoning, and Novel Neural Network Architectures.

MSK-Net: Multi-Scale Spatial KANs Enhanced U-Shape Network for Explainable 3D Brain Tumor Segmentation Yutong Wang, Zhongfeng Kang, et al. Knowledge-Based Systems, 2026. SCI Q1 Top · IF 8.0 Accepted Keywords: Kolmogorov–Arnold Networks · 3D Brain Tumor Segmentation · Spatial KAN · Cross-Scale Fusion · Explainable AI. Core innovations: Spatial KAN blocks that lift voxel neighborhoods into learnable nonlinear bases; KAN-based gated attention for tumor-relevant region selection; cross-scale gated fusion preserving boundary detail while keeping the decision path interpretable.
HMP-Net: A Hierarchical Multi-Prior Network for Brain Tumor Segmentation Integrating Physics, Topology, and Tumor Dynamics Yutong Wang, Zhongfeng Kang, Jiaxue Yang, Shantian Yang, Qinghua Zhao, Zichen Song. Neurocomputing, Vol. 691, 133827, 2026. SCI Q1 · IF 6.7 Keywords: Brain Tumor Segmentation · Hierarchical Multi-Prior Fusion · MRI Physics Encoding · Differentiable Topology · Reaction–Diffusion Dynamics. Core innovations: Hierarchical injection of physical, topological, and dynamical priors into a single end-to-end network; differentiable Betti numbers as a topology-aware regularizer; Fisher–Kolmogorov reaction–diffusion modeling of tumor growth to constrain spatial predictions.
KAN-Boosted Chinese Online Abuse Detection Framework with Sentiment and Toxicity Fusion through Global-Local-Differential Attention Yutong Wang, Zhongfeng Kang, Jiaxue Yang, Xiaopeng Fan, Zijin Wu, Shantian Yang, Qinghua Zhao, Zichen Song. Expert Systems with Applications, 132732, 2026. SCI Q1 Top · IF 9.4 Keywords: Chinese Toxicity Detection · Kolmogorov–Arnold Networks · Sentiment–Toxicity Fusion · Global-Local-Differential Attention · BERT/RoBERTa Dual Encoder. Core innovations: Dual BERT/RoBERTa encoder that disentangles sentiment cues from explicit toxicity signals; Global-Local-Differential Attention to capture cross-granularity disagreement between the two streams; KAN classification head that sharpens the decision boundary on ambiguous abusive expressions (Macro-F1 82.56% on COLDataset).
When Comments Aren’t What They Seem: The Social Media Comment Toxicity Detector for Understanding Contextual Comments Zichen Song*, Xiaopeng Fan, Yutong Wang*, Feixuan Yan, Zijin Wu, Zhongfeng Kang. (*Co-first authors) Expert Systems with Applications, Vol. 305, 130902, 2026. SCI Q1 Top · IF 9.4 Keywords: Social Media Moderation · In-Context Learning · LLM-Driven Dialogue Expansion · Contextual Toxicity Detection · GPT Reasoning. Core innovations: Reformulates isolated comments as GPT-simulated multi-turn reply threads so latent conversational context becomes explicit input; an ICL framework whose prompts are conditioned on the expanded thread structure; outperforms Perspective API by +4.8% on YouTube comment streams.
ResKANNet: A Residual Kolmogorov–Arnold Network with Multi-Scale Attention for Brain Tumor Segmentation Zhengyi Li, Zhongfeng Kang, Yutong Wang, Xinyu Kang, Shantian Yang, Qinghua Zhao. Neurocomputing, Vol. 664, 132062, 2026. SCI Q1 · IF 6.7 Keywords: Residual Kolmogorov–Arnold Networks · Brain Tumor Segmentation · Multi-Scale Attention · EMCAM/CBAM3D · 3D U-Net. Core innovations: Selective KAN placement only at high-semantic layers to bound parameter cost; residual 3D U-shape backbone for stable gradient flow on volumetric data; hybrid EMCAM + CBAM3D attention that aggregates channel and multi-scale spatial cues (90.95% Dice on BraTS2021).
